Intro

The third meeting of the Calendar Reform Committee was held on the 13th Sept. 1954 at 10 A.M. in the C.S.I.R. Building, New Delhi.

  1. The members present signed the final report for submission.

The following members were present :

    1. Prof. M. N. Saha, Chairman
    1. Shri J. S. Karandikar, Member
    1. Prof. A. C. Banerji,
    1. Dr. Gorakh Prasad,
    1. Prof. R. V. Vaidya,
    1. Shri N. C. Lahiri,
  • Dr. Daftari in letters to Dr. Gorakh Prasad and to Shri Karandikar expressed his inability to attend due to reasons of health.

  1. The proceedings of the last meeting were read and confirmed.

  2. The introductory portion of the report and the final recommendations and also the experimental National Calendar prepared for five years were read, and scrutinized by all the members and were approved after small corrections.

  3. It was resolved that the Government be requested to print Parts A and B immediately for circulation and eliciting public opinion. When Part C will be completed and approved by the members of the Committee, it will also have to be printed, and circulated.

  4. A letter from the Chairman regarding his visit of the Nautical Almanac Office at Herstmonceux (England) was read. It was resolved that the travelling expenses from London to Herstmonceux and back incurred should be borne by the Calendar Reform Committee.

  5. The members requested the Chairman to move the Government for making arrangement for publishing an ‘Indian Ephemeris and Nautical Almanac’ for India.

  6. The Chairman then thanked the members for the trouble they had taken and the interest shown in the smooth working of the Committee.
